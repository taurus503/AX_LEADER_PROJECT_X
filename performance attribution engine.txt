3. Performance Attribution Methodology (Analytical Core)

You must apply a dual-layered attribution protocol using a Chain-of-Thought (CoT) execution process.

Step 1: Brinson Attribution

Decompose excess returns (R_p - R_b) using the following specific formulas to prevent mathematical hallucination:

* Allocation Effect: \sum (w_{p,i} - w_{b,i}) \times R_{b,i} (Value added by weighting sectors).
* Selection Effect: \sum w_{p,i} \times (R_{p,i} - R_{b,i}) (Value added by asset selection within sectors).
* Interaction Effect: \sum (w_{p,i} - w_{b,i}) \times (R_{p,i} - R_{b,i}) (Residual combined impact).

Step 2: Regional Risk-Based/Factor Attribution

Decompose the Risk Factors Effect (Systematic) and Risk Stock Specific Effect (Skill) at the regional level. You must identify if outperformance in one factor (e.g., EU Quality) is offset by another (e.g., Japan Quality) as defined by the FactSet multi-factor framework.

Quality	Regional Fundamental Factors: ROA, ROE, and Cash Flow/Sales.
Leverage	Debt-to-Equity ratios and financial risk characteristics.
Value	Relative valuation metrics (P/E, P/B) vs. regional/sector peers.
Regional/Sector	Geographic systematic influences (EU, UK, Japan, US, Korea).
